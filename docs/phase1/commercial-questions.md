# Phase 1 Commercial Questions For Martis MV

Purpose:

Before development starts, Martis MV must confirm how the website will make money, who owns Stripe, and what counts as a website-generated job.

These questions are written in simple English for a Slovak-speaking business owner.

## 1. Commission Model

Please confirm this agreement:

- DCZ WebAgentura receives 35% from paid jobs generated by the website.
- This continues only until the total commission reaches EUR 1,000.
- After EUR 1,000 total commission is paid, the website development is considered repaid.

Question:

- Do you agree with this 35% commission model up to EUR 1,000 total?

Answer needed:

- Yes / No / Needs change

## 2. What Counts As A Website-Generated Job?

We need to define exactly when a job is counted as coming from the website.

Possible sources:

- Customer sends contact form.
- Customer clicks phone number from the website and later orders work.
- Customer clicks WhatsApp from the website and sends photos.
- Customer books an inspection through the website.
- Customer pays a reservation fee or consultation fee through Stripe.

Question:

- Which of these should count as a website-generated job?

Recommended answer:

- Count all of them, but Stripe payment and booking are the strongest proof.
- For phone and WhatsApp, the job should count only if the customer can be matched by phone number, name or message.

## 3. Stripe Account Ownership

Stripe can be owned by Martis MV or by DCZ WebAgentura.

Question:

- Who will own the Stripe account?

Options:

- Martis MV owns Stripe. Martis MV receives payments directly and handles accounting/refunds.
- DCZ WebAgentura owns Stripe temporarily. DCZ receives payments and must later settle/account with Martis MV.

Recommended answer:

- Martis MV owns Stripe, because payments, refunds, accounting and customer receipts belong to the business providing the service.

Follow-up questions:

- Who will handle refunds?
- Who will handle Stripe payout/accounting questions?
- Who will receive Stripe notification emails?

## 4. Reservation Fee Amount

The reservation fee is a small payment for a serious inspection request.

Recommended default:

- EUR 30

Question:

- Should the reservation fee be EUR 30, or another amount?

Answer needed:

- EUR 30 / Other amount

## 5. Paid Consultation Fee Amount

The consultation fee is for more serious renovation advice, planning or expert consultation.

Recommended default:

- EUR 80

Question:

- Should the paid consultation fee be EUR 80, or another amount?

Answer needed:

- EUR 80 / Other amount

## 6. Project Deposits

A project deposit is a larger payment after the customer approves an offer.

Question:

- Should project deposits be enabled at launch?

Options:

- Enable at launch.
- Prepare technically, but activate only after the first real client workflow is tested.

Recommended answer:

- Prepare technically, but activate only after the first real client workflow. This keeps MVP launch safer and avoids accounting/refund confusion.

## 7. Commission Records

We need a way to see website leads, payments and commission evidence.

Question:

- How should commission records be viewed?

Options:

- Simple private admin view.
- Export only, for example CSV from database.

Recommended answer:

- Start with export only for MVP. Add a simple private admin view later if it saves real time.

## 8. Required Client Materials

Please prepare:

- Company name exactly as it should appear.
- Phone number.
- Email address.
- WhatsApp number.
- Billing details.
- Service area list.
- Service priority list.
- Real work photos.
- References or completed jobs.
- Domain access.
- Google Business Profile access.
- Stripe account decision.
